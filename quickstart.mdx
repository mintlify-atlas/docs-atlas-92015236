---
title: Quickstart
description: Get up and running with Ophira Web in minutes — log in, configure a location and category, create your first asset, and scan its QR code.
---

This guide walks you through the first things you need to do after your account is created: logging in, orienting yourself in the app, setting up the structure your assets will live in, creating an asset, and scanning it with the built-in QR scanner.

<Info>
You need an existing account to log in. Accounts are created by an Admin user from the **Users** section. If you do not have credentials yet, contact your system administrator.
</Info>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log in to Ophira Web">
    Open Ophira Web in your browser. You will land on the login page.

    1. Enter your **email address** in the Email field.
    2. Enter your **password** in the Password field.
    3. If you are on a personal device and want your session to persist after closing the browser, check **Recuérdame** (Remember me). Leave it unchecked on shared or public devices.
    4. Click **Iniciar Sesión**.

    On a successful login, you are redirected to the Dashboard.

    <Note>
    If you check "Remember me", your session token is stored in `localStorage` and persists across browser restarts. If you leave it unchecked, the token is stored in `sessionStorage` and is cleared when you close the tab or browser. See [Authentication](/authentication) for full details.
    </Note>

    If you forgot your password, click **¿Olvidaste tu contraseña?** on the login page. Enter your email address and click **Enviar correo** — this opens your default mail client with a pre-drafted password reset request addressed to the system administrator.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Understand the sidebar navigation">
    After logging in, you will see a fixed left sidebar labelled **Ophira QR**. The sidebar contains all the main sections of the application:

    | Sidebar item | What it does |
    |---|---|
    | **Dashboard** | Summary stats, recent activity, and quick actions for locations and categories |
    | **Activos** | Full asset registry with search and filters |
    | **Leer QR** | Camera-based QR code scanner |
    | **Reportes** | Reports and exports |
    | **Historial** | Asset movement history log |
    | **Auditorias** | Audit session management |
    | **Usuarios** | User account management |

    At the bottom of the sidebar you can see the logged-in user's name and email address, a button to toggle between light and dark mode, and a **Cerrar sesión** button to sign out.

    <Note>
    The current version of Ophira Web requires the Administrador role to access any page. Once logged in as an Admin, all sidebar navigation items are visible and accessible.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set up a building and category">
    Before creating assets, you need at least one location (a room inside a building) and at least one category.

    **Create a building**

    1. Go to **Dashboard**.
    2. Click **Nueva Ubicacion** in the top-right area of the Dashboard header.
    3. A panel opens with three tabs. Start with **Nuevo edificio**.
    4. Fill in the fields:
       - **Clave** — a short identifier for the building (e.g., `A`, `B`, `LAB`)
       - **Nombre** — the building's full name (e.g., `Edificio Administrativo`)
       - **Cantidad de pisos** — total number of floors in the building
       - **Direccion** — the physical address of the building
    5. Click **Crear edificio**.

    **Create a floor**

    1. Still in the **Nueva Ubicacion** panel, click **Nuevo piso**.
    2. Select the building you just created.
    3. Choose the floor number from the dropdown.
    4. Select how many rooms are on that floor.
    5. Click **Crear piso**.

    **Create a room**

    1. Click **Nueva aula** in the same panel.
    2. Select the building, then the floor.
    3. Choose the room number and enter the room type (e.g., `Aula`, `Laboratorio`, `Oficina`).
    4. Click **Crear aula**.

    **Create a category**

    1. On the Dashboard, find the **Distribución por Categoría** card.
    2. Click the three-dot menu icon in the top-right corner of that card.
    3. Enter a **Nombre de categoria** (e.g., `Electrónicos`) and a **Descripcion breve**.
    4. Click **Crear categoria**.

    <Note>
    Only users with Admin or Supervisor roles can create buildings, floors, rooms, and categories. If you do not see the **Nueva Ubicacion** button or the category menu, your role does not have permission to perform these actions.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create your first asset">
    1. Click **Nuevo Activo** in the Dashboard header, or navigate to **Activos** in the sidebar and click the **Nuevo Activo** button there.
    2. You are taken to the asset creation form. Fill in the required fields:

       | Field | Description |
       |---|---|
       | **Nombre del Activo** | The asset's name (e.g., `Dell Latitude 5520`) |
       | **Número de Serie** | Serial number |
       | **Modelo** | Model identifier |
       | **Fecha de Compra** | Purchase date |
       | **Precio de Compra** | Original purchase price in MXN |
       | **Valor Actual** | Current estimated value in MXN |
       | **Valor Residual** | Estimated residual value at end of useful life in MXN |
       | **Vida Útil (años)** | Expected useful life in years |
       | **Categoría** | The category you created in the previous step |
       | **Método de Depreciación** | Depreciation method (loaded from the system) |
       | **Estado** | Current asset status (loaded from the system) |
       | **Aula en que se ubica** | The room where the asset is physically located |
       | **Responsable** | The user responsible for this asset |
       | **Descripción** | A brief description of the asset |

    3. If the asset has multiple physical parts stored in different rooms, enable the **Multiparte** toggle and add each part with its own room assignment.
    4. Click **Guardar** to save the asset.

    After saving, the asset is assigned a unique numeric ID and a QR code is generated automatically. You can view and download the QR code from the asset detail page.

    <Tip>
    The Dashboard's **Actividad Reciente** table updates immediately after you save a new asset. You can also use **Activos del Día** on the Dashboard to export a PDF of all assets registered today, including their QR codes.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Scan the asset's QR code">
    1. Navigate to **Leer QR** in the sidebar.
    2. Your browser will request camera access — allow it.
    3. Point the camera at the QR code on the asset you just created.
    4. The scanner reads the QR code automatically and looks up the asset by its ID.
    5. The asset's details appear below the camera view: name, category, status, responsible user, and location.
    6. The scan is recorded as a movement of type `escaneo` in the asset's history.

    If your camera is not available or you are testing without a physical QR code, enter the asset's numeric ID in the **Busqueda manual** field on the Scanner page and press the search button.

    <Note>
    Every scan creates a movement record linked to your user account. This record appears in the **Historial** module and is visible in the **Auditorias** module when an audit session is active for that location.
    </Note>
  </Step>
</Steps>
